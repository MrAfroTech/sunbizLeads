Prom & Graduation Season — The Fan Acquisition Window
THOUSANDS OF NEW FANS IN YOUR MARKET RIGHT NOW.
MOST TEAMS WON'T CAPTURE ONE.

"Prom and graduation season is the biggest new-fan acquisition window of the year. Most teams let it close without capturing a single contact."

Graduation season runs roughly six weeks. In that window, families are celebrating, students are transitioning, and a group of people who might not have thought about your team in months are suddenly in a mood to spend money on experiences.

That attention doesn't stick on its own. Gen Z fans — who index highest during preseason at 74% tune-in — are the most front-loaded audience in sports. They're paying attention early. If you don't capture them now, you're trying to win them back mid-season when the noise is louder and the competition for their time is steeper.

The question isn't whether graduation season is a good moment. It clearly is. The question is whether you have anything set up to turn that moment into a repeat fan.

WHY THIS WINDOW IS DIFFERENT FROM ANY OTHER
Families Are Already in a Celebration Mindset. That Changes Everything.
01
THE AUDIENCE SHIFT
Gen Z Tunes In Early — Then Drifts If You Don't Hold Them.
74% of Gen Z fans are most engaged during preseason. Their attention is front-loaded. Graduation season lands right in that window. If your only touchpoint is game-day content, you're missing the period when this audience is most open to becoming a fan.
02
THE FAMILY ANGLE
Graduation Brings Families Who Don't Usually Show Up Together.
Graduation weekends pull extended families into the same city, often looking for something to do together beyond the ceremony dinner. A family that attends one game during graduation weekend isn't just one ticket — it's a group experience that, with the right follow-up, can become a recurring one.
03
THE CREATOR FACTOR
Gen Z Fans Are Shaped By Authenticity, Not Ads.
Sports fandom for younger audiences is increasingly built around behind-the-scenes content, player personality, and short-form video — not broadcast. Graduation season is a natural hook for that kind of content. A team celebrating its own graduating student-athletes, for example, lands differently than a generic promotional post.
04
THE STORYLINE
NCAA Division I Student-Athletes Are Graduating at a Record 91%.
That's not just a stat — it's a content angle. Celebrating your athletes as people who finished something difficult resonates with families in a graduation mindset. It's the kind of authentic storyline that performs on social without feeling like a campaign.

Older fans are different. Their engagement builds toward playoffs and finals. Those are two separate conversations, and most teams send one.

WHAT THE GAP LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE
Same Graduation Window. Two Different Outcomes.
The difference is whether you have a system to capture the moment or just experience it.
WITHOUT IT
A family comes to a game during graduation weekend. Great night. They leave. You don't know who they were. Graduation season ends and you're back to your baseline audience with nothing added.
WITH IT
Same family comes in. Their interaction is captured. You know they were a graduation-weekend group. Next season, you have a reason to reach out with a family package before the first home game. That's a repeat fan, not a one-time visit.
WITHOUT IT
Gen Z fans engage with your social content during preseason, click once, then drift when the next thing comes along. No capture, no follow-up, no conversion.
WITH IT
That same fan scans something — at the venue, at a pop-up, through a low-friction entry point — and you have a way to reach them again when the season actually starts. Early attention becomes a trackable relationship.
WHERE THE OPPORTUNITY IS SITTING RIGHT NOW
Four Graduation-Season Moments Teams Are Leaving Uncaptured.
FAMILY GROUPS
Graduation weekends bring extended families looking for group experiences. A team that offers a family package — easy to find, easy to book, frictionless entry — is positioned to capture a group that came for the graduation and stayed for the game. The hard part isn't selling the ticket. It's making sure you have a way to reach them again afterward.
GEN Z FANS
74% of Gen Z fans are most engaged right now, before the season peaks. That engagement is real, but it's short. Behind-the-scenes content, student-athlete stories, and social-first moments are what hold attention in this window. The teams that convert Gen Z fans are the ones treating early engagement as a capture opportunity, not just a view count.
STUDENT-ATHLETE STORIES
Division I athletes are graduating at 91% — a record. That's not a press release stat. It's a human story that resonates with families in a graduation mindset. A post about a player who just finished their degree, told honestly, performs differently than a highlight reel. It also reaches people who aren't yet your fans.
LOW-FRICTION ENTRY
New families and younger fans need a simple first step. A group ticket offer, a scan-to-enter promotion, a social post with a clear and easy next action — whatever removes the friction between "I'm interested" and "I'm in." The teams converting graduation-season attention are the ones making that first step obvious and uncomplicated.

Graduation season isn't a marketing campaign. It's a six-week window where a specific type of fan — families, students, Gen Z — is already in a spending and celebrating mindset. The teams that build something lasting from it are the ones who treat attendance as the beginning of a relationship, not the end of a transaction.
