COMES NOW, Petitioner, MAURICE SANDERS, pro se, and respectfully moves this Court for Emergency Temporary Child Support, and in support thereof states:

BACKGROUND
1. Petitioner is the primary caregiver of the minor child, A.K.S., born August 13, 2016, and has been the sole consistent primary caregiver for approximately five (5) years.
2. No child support order currently exists between the parties.
3. Respondent, VERUSHKA WILLIAMS, currently resides in the State of Texas while the minor child resides with Petitioner at 21725 CR-33, Lot 16, Groveland, FL 34736.
4. Paternity was legally established by Final Judgment of Paternity entered February 18, 2021.
PETITIONER'S DEMONSTRATED COMMITMENT TO THE CHILD'S STABILITY
5. When Respondent relinquished physical custody of the minor child to Petitioner in or around November 2020, Petitioner was experiencing a period of housing instability. Despite this challenge, Petitioner accepted full parental responsibility for the child without hesitation and immediately provided for the child's daily needs.
6. When this Court granted Petitioner primary parenting time in February 2021, Petitioner was similarly navigating a period of housing instability. Notwithstanding these circumstances, this Court found Petitioner fit and appropriate to serve as the child's primary custodial parent — a determination Petitioner has honored without interruption for five (5) years.
7. In the five (5) years since, Petitioner has provided the minor child with consistent, stable, and nurturing care and has served as the child's sole source of daily support, guidance, and stability. Petitioner's history reflects not instability, but extraordinary resilience and an unwavering commitment to this child under difficult circumstances.
8. Respondent was fully aware of Petitioner's circumstances at the time she voluntarily relinquished custody in November 2020 and at the time this Court awarded primary parenting time in February 2021. Respondent cannot now invoke Petitioner's financial hardship as a basis for custody modification when she had full knowledge of these circumstances at the outset and this Court has already adjudicated Petitioner's fitness as primary custodial parent.
RESPONDENT'S INCONSISTENT AND INSUFFICIENT FINANCIAL CONTRIBUTION
9. Over the course of the five (5) years Petitioner has served as primary caregiver, Respondent's financial contributions to the minor child's daily needs have been sporadic, inconsistent, and wholly insufficient to meet the child's ongoing and recurring needs. Respondent has contributed on her own terms and timeline rather than in any reliable or consistent manner.
10. Respondent has made clear through her written communications, verbal statements, and conduct that she will only provide consistent financial support if and when ordered to do so by this Court. (See Exhibit A.) The minor child should not be made to suffer the consequences of Respondent's unwillingness to voluntarily fulfill her parental financial obligations.
11. Petitioner has made multiple written requests to Respondent for financial assistance toward the minor child's day-to-day needs, including but not limited to transportation to school, before and after school childcare, food, clothing, and other daily living expenses. Copies of these written communications are attached hereto as Exhibit A.
12. Respondent has refused to consistently contribute to before and after school childcare expenses, thereby placing the recurring financial burden of the child's daily care on Petitioner alone.
13. In lieu of contributing to the child's ongoing daily needs, Respondent indicated she would "cross-reference" the one-time cost of back-to-school clothing she purchased as a financial offset. A single, one-time clothing purchase does not address or substitute for the child's recurring daily transportation, childcare, and living expenses.
14. In response to Petitioner's requests for financial assistance, Respondent stated in writing: "Just like I have to go through the courts to request any modifications to the parenting plan due to your continued violations of the order, I suggest you do the same. The current order remains in effect unless and until the court modifies it, and any substantial changes must be addressed through the proper legal process." (See Exhibit A.) While Petitioner fully respects the legal process and files this Motion accordingly, Respondent's written response confirms her awareness of Petitioner's financial hardship and documents her deliberate choice to withhold consistent financial support absent a court order compelling her to provide it.
IMPACT ON PETITIONER'S EMPLOYMENT AND EARNING CAPACITY
15. Respondent's failure to consistently contribute to before and after school childcare has directly and materially impacted Petitioner's ability to maintain stable employment and earn consistent income.
16. Due to the absence of reliable before and after school childcare — a direct consequence of Respondent's inconsistent financial contributions — Petitioner is currently limited to accepting employment only during school hours. This restriction significantly constrains Petitioner's earning capacity and his ability to provide for the minor child's daily needs.
17. Respondent's refusal to consistently fund childcare according to the child's actual care schedule — rather than on Respondent's own preferred payment schedule — has created unreliable childcare arrangements that have directly interfered with Petitioner's work obligations and resulted in lost wages and reduced working hours.
18. This financial cascade — from inconsistent childcare contributions, to disrupted childcare arrangements, to lost employment hours, to reduced income — traces directly to Respondent's conduct and has materially diminished Petitioner's ability to meet the minor child's daily needs, including transportation to school. Respondent cannot simultaneously cause this chain of financial harm and invoke it as evidence of Petitioner's unfitness.
TRAVEL OBLIGATIONS, FINANCIAL HARDSHIP, AND RESPONDENT'S PATTERN OF CONDUCT
19. Petitioner acknowledges that pursuant to the existing parenting plan, Petitioner bears responsibility for the cost of transporting the minor child to Texas for Respondent's designated parenting time.
20. Petitioner was unable to fulfill this travel obligation during the winter break parenting period due to a brief incarceration of forty-three (43) days — a circumstance beyond Petitioner's control and not a willful violation of the parenting plan.
21. Regarding the upcoming spring break parenting time, Petitioner requested that Respondent offset the cost of travel against the substantial day-to-day expenses for the child's food, transportation, childcare, and daily living needs that Respondent has declined to consistently contribute toward. This was a reasonable and good-faith proposal to equitably address both parties' financial obligations to the child.
22. Rather than engaging with Petitioner's proposed offset or agreeing to contribute to the child's daily expenses, Respondent subsequently offered to travel to Florida to retrieve the child herself — an offer made only after Petitioner raised the question of financial offset, and one that conveniently avoids any financial transfer to Petitioner or acknowledgment of the day-to-day expenses Petitioner has borne alone.
23. Respondent has not yet executed this offer or provided any financial contribution toward the child's daily needs. This pattern — refusing consistent financial support while simultaneously citing the parenting plan to assert violations — reflects a deliberate and inequitable course of conduct that this Court should consider in any determination regarding support and custody.
24. Petitioner respectfully requests that this Court address the equitable allocation of travel costs and the outstanding day-to-day expenses as part of any temporary or permanent support order entered herein.

36.
