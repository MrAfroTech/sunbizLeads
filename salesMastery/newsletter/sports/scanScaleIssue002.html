The Fan Data Gap
8 IN 10 FANS WILL SHARE DATA.
MOST TEAMS DO NOTHING WITH IT.

"Your fans are already willing to tell you who they are. The question is whether you're set up to listen."

More than 80% of consumers will share data via QR code — either by opting in directly or through an opt-out flow. That number isn't a maybe. It's been consistent across multiple independent studies.

Yet fewer than 36% of marketers actually use QR codes to deliver a personalized follow-up experience. In sports, the gap is even wider. Most stadiums use QR codes to solve one problem — get people through the gate or to a digital menu — and stop there.

The scan happens. Nothing follows. The fan goes home. You don't know who they were.

That's the 80% rule. Most of your fans are willing to give you something. Most teams aren't built to receive it.

WHAT'S ACTUALLY HAPPENING IN YOUR STADIUM
The Problem Isn't QR Adoption. It's What Comes After.
01
THE COLLECTION GAP
You're Counting Scans. Not Building Fan Profiles.
90%+ of marketers say QR codes are valuable for first-party data collection. Most stadiums track scan volume. That's not a fan profile — that's a page view. Scan count doesn't tell you who will renew their season tickets.
02
THE INTENT GAP
75% of Fans Scan to Get More Information. You're Not Giving Them Any.
The top reason fans scan QR codes is to get more information. The second is discounts. Most stadium QR codes deliver a static menu or a ticketing link. That's not information. It's a redirect. There's no preference capture, no follow-up, no memory of the interaction.
03
THE REVENUE GAP
A One-Time Ticket Buyer Is Not a Season Ticket Holder.
QR data — if collected and acted on — can tell you which fans come back, what they buy, what they care about, and which ones are drifting. Without it, every renewal campaign is a cold guess. With it, you know exactly who to target and what to say.
04
THE TRUST GAP
Fans Trust QR Codes More Than They Did Last Year.
Nearly 60% of consumers say they're confident QR codes are safe to scan. Over 25% trust them more now than 12 months ago. The barrier to opt-in is lower than it's ever been. The only thing missing is a system that uses what fans are already willing to share.

WHAT FAN INTELLIGENCE ACTUALLY LOOKS LIKE
Same QR Code. Completely Different Outcome.
The difference isn't the scan. It's what your system does next.
WITHOUT IT
Fan scans a QR code at the concession stand. Gets a menu. Orders or doesn't. You have no idea who they are, what they like, or whether they'll be back next week.
WITH IT
Fan scans. System captures preference — what they ordered, when, how often they've been in. Next game, they get a relevant offer tied to their history. You know who your repeat fans are before renewal season starts.
WITHOUT IT
Renewal campaigns go to every name in the database with the same message. Conversion rates are inconsistent and hard to explain.
WITH IT
You can identify lapsed fans — someone who hasn't scanned in three games — and reach out with a specific, relevant reason to come back. Not a blast. A targeted interaction.
WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE INSIDE YOUR VENUE
Four Places Where Fan Intelligence Changes the Outcome.
CONCESSIONS
Fan scans at a food stand. System knows they ordered wings last game. This game they get a relevant offer tied to what they already bought — not a generic discount, a specific one. Purchase history drives the offer. That's the difference between a promotion and a personalized interaction.
MERCHANDISE
Fan scans a jersey tag at the team store. System connects their scan history to a favorite player they've interacted with before. Shows them the next home game date and a relevant bundle offer. The scan becomes a conversion point instead of a browse.
ENTRY & PARKING
Fan scans at gate entry. System knows their visit frequency and usual arrival pattern. Over time, you understand peak arrival behavior across your entire fan base. That's operational data that informs staffing, security deployment, and lane design — not just a head count.
FAN RETENTION
Fan misses three games in a row. System flags them as lapsed. Your team reaches out with a specific reason to return — not a mass email, a targeted message tied to their fan history. That's the difference between a renewal strategy and a retention system.

The point isn't that QR codes are new. 94% of sports marketers already increased QR usage last year. The point is that adding more QR codes without a system behind them just gives you more scans. More scans aren't more fans.
