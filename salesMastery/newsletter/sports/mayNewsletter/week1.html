"The summer concert and season ticket slate is already being built. The venues with the best fan data are filling premium inventory now. The ones without it are discounting at the gate and wondering why."

Right now, your fans are deciding where they're spending their summers — which games they'll attend, which venues they'll commit to, which experiences are worth the ticket price. And what they're looking for, more than a great lineup or a prime seat location, is a venue that feels like it actually knows them.

73% of sports and live event attendees say a personalized experience increases their likelihood of returning to the same venue. Not a nice touch. A revenue driver.

That's a decisive number. The operator who sends the right offer to the right fan at the right moment — based on their actual history — has a massive advantage over the one blasting the same promo to every name on the list.

The technology making this possible is accelerating. Platforms that can read your fan behavior — purchase history, concession preferences, seat location patterns — and serve up targeted offers in real time are being adopted at a 35% faster rate across mid-size arenas and event venues than just a year ago.

This isn't just for the NFL and NBA. Independent arenas, amphitheaters, and multi-use event venues can do this too. You don't need an enterprise budget. You need to start activating the data that's already sitting in your systems.

Your POS knows what fans order and when. Your ticketing platform knows their section, their visit frequency, and their spend tier. Your event history knows what drove them back. That's a conversion engine. Most venues aren't using it.

What You Can Do Right Now
Start Simple. Start Now.
Three Moves For This Month

Audit your fan data touchpoints. Pull your POS, ticketing, and mobile ordering data into one view. What do your highest-spend fans buy? Which sections drive the most concession volume? That's your personalization starting point.

Segment your fan list by behavior. No advanced platform required. Separate season ticket holders from single-game buyers, high-spend from low-spend, early arrivals from gate-time arrivals. Each group gets a message built for them — not a generic blast.

Launch a summer event preview campaign this month. Fans are booking now. A targeted "here's what's coming to our venue this summer" push — with an early access or package offer — can move premium inventory weeks before your first event doors open.
