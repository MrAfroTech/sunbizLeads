Fan Experience & Revenue

Fans Bail on 8-Minute Concession Lines

Long lines cost money and frustrate fans. Here's what the data shows across stadiums and major events.

stadiumPhotos/photo1.jpeg

The Scale of the Problem

Half of Fans Abandon at Least One Line Per Game

Across stadiums and major events, data shows roughly half of all fans abandon at least one line per game — F&B, merch, and will-call lines all see drop-offs. The revenue never comes back once a fan walks away.

50% of fans abandon at least one line per game · F&B, merch, and will-call all affected
The 8-Minute Threshold

Fans Spend 20% More When Waits Stay Under 8 Minutes

The data is consistent: shorter lines equal more revenue. Fans make fast purchasing decisions at events. When the perceived wait crosses the 8-minute mark, many don't join the line at all.

Shorter lines don't just improve satisfaction. They directly increase per-cap spend by up to 20%.

Common guest behaviors when lines look too long:

  • Searching for a shorter line elsewhere in the venue
  • Deciding to skip the purchase entirely and return to their seat
  • Missing plays while waiting — reducing engagement and satisfaction

stadiumPhotos/photo2.jpeg

Every abandoned line is a permanent revenue loss — the fan returns to their seat, not the line
The Revenue Math

20–30% of Peak Transactions Are Lost to Line Abandonment

During peak periods, line abandonment causes 20–30% of potential transactions to disappear. For a single game, that can translate to $100K–$300K in unrealized revenue — not from pricing, but from friction.

$300K possible single-game revenue loss from line abandonment at peak periods
20–30% of peak transactions lost when lines exceed guest tolerance thresholds
Mobile Ordering Isn't a Silver Bullet

Digital Convenience Only Works When Fulfillment Is Fast

Mobile ordering fails if pickup takes 10+ minutes. The ordering friction moves from the line to the pickup window — and the guest experience suffers regardless of how slick the app is.

stadiumPhotos/photo3.jpeg

Mobile ordering solves half the problem — fulfillment speed solves the other half
The Takeaway

Faster lines increase revenue, capture lost sales, and improve the fan experience.

Nearly 80% of fans miss at least one play per game waiting in line. The opportunity isn't in the menu or the pricing — it's in reducing the time between decision and transaction.

The revenue is already in the stadium. Friction is the only thing standing between it and your bottom line.
