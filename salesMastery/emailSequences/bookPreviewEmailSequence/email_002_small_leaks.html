The Compound Effect

Follow The Money
Small Leaks.
Catastrophic Losses.

"No single moment kills the night. It's five moments that each feel like nothing — until you add them up across 6,000 guests and 40 home games."

Last email I told you about friction. This one is about compounding.

The reason most venues can't see the problem is because none of the individual moments look like a crisis. A 4-minute wait at the entry scanner. A concession stand that runs out of a high-margin item by the third inning. A payment terminal that goes down for 11 minutes during peak halftime rush. A guest who can't find their seat and spends 8 minutes wandering instead of spending.

None of those feel like emergencies. But run them across a full house, night after night, and you're not dealing with a leak — you're dealing with a drain.

Where The Money Goes — Moment By Moment
The Leak Map
Moment 01 — Entry
The Line Before Anything Happens
Guests who wait more than 8 minutes at entry arrive frustrated. Their first purchase — which sets the spending pattern for the night — drops 22% compared to guests who walked straight in. You haven't served them a drink yet and they're already spending less.
Avg. impact: −$6.20 / guest
Moment 02 — First Purchase
The Menu Nobody Reads Fast
If a guest can't decide in under 90 seconds at the counter, they default to something cheap or nothing. Confusing menus, items listed without prices, no upsell prompts — every one of those is a margin decision made against you. The concession stand that sorted its top 5 sellers onto a single board saw average ticket size climb 18% in one season.
Avg. impact: −$4.50 / transaction
Moment 03 — Mid-Event
The Second Purchase That Never Happens
The highest-spending guests — your top quartile — often buy twice. But the second purchase only happens if they can get back to a concession point fast. If the line at halftime takes more than 6 minutes, 40% of those guests give up and wait until they leave. They don't cancel the purchase. They just never make it.
Avg. impact: −$9.80 / high-intent guest
Moment 04 — Exit Intent
The Guest Who Doesn't Come Back
This one has no dollar amount on the night. But the guest who felt like the venue was hard to navigate, hard to buy from, or hard to enjoy — their return intent drops sharply. National data puts non-return rates at 2.4× higher among guests who experienced two or more friction points in a single visit.
LTV impact: −$140–$310 / guest

That's not a guess pulled from thin air. That's what falls out when you apply the compound rate across a real mid-market venue. Some of your venues are losing less. Some are losing more. But almost none of them have sat down and mapped it this way — moment by moment, leak by leak, multiplied by volume.

The operators who fix this don't do it all at once. They pick the highest-impact moment, fix it, measure it, then move to the next. That's what a system looks like. That's what Chaos Mastery is about.

From Chaos Mastery — Chapter 9: Waiting

You recover spend that was already lost.
