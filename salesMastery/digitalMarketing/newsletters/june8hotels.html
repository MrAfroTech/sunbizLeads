Speed Is Revenue — The Arrival Gap Shaping Your Guest's Entire Stay
YOUR GUESTS DECIDE HOW THEY FEEL ABOUT YOUR HOTEL
IN THE FIRST TWO MINUTES.

"The front desk line isn't just a wait. It's the first impression of every single stay — and it's setting the emotional baseline for everything that follows."

The check-in moment is different from almost every other service interaction in hospitality. A guest waiting at a restaurant bar is still inside the experience — they're surrounded by ambiance, drinks, people. A guest standing at a hotel front desk queue is in a holding pattern. They haven't started their stay yet. And every minute they wait is a minute they're building a slightly worse baseline mood for the hours ahead.

The target that hospitality technology operators are now benchmarking against is 2 minutes for the arrival flow. That's the expectation that's forming among guests — particularly the 82% of Gen Z hotel travelers who already prefer app or kiosk check-in to the front desk. The properties that get there aren't just winning on satisfaction scores. They're freeing their staff to do the parts of hospitality that can't be automated, and they're setting up guests to spend more throughout the stay.

Speed at check-in isn't the whole picture. But it is the beginning of it. Get the arrival right and everything downstream — F&B spend, amenity usage, ancillary bookings — performs better.

WHY ARRIVAL SPEED IS THE HIGHEST-LEVERAGE TOUCHPOINT IN HOTEL OPERATIONS
Four Reasons Faster Check-In Compounds Into Revenue.
01
THE 2-MINUTE BENCHMARK
The New Guest Expectation for Arrival Flow Is 2 Minutes. Most Hotels Are Still at 6+.
Hospitality tech benchmarks now point to 2 minutes as the guest expectation for a frictionless arrival. Contactless check-in processes are saving 4 to 6 minutes per interaction compared to traditional front desk flows. That gap between expectation and current performance is where guest satisfaction scores are being won and lost before the room is ever seen.
02
THE GEN Z PREFERENCE
82% of Gen Z Hotel Guests Prefer Digital Check-In. They're Also Your Fastest-Growing Segment.
Gen Z hotel travelers aren't a niche — they're the demographic that's going to drive hospitality revenue for the next two decades. 82% of them prefer app or kiosk check-in to standing at a front desk. Properties that still rely exclusively on traditional arrival flows are creating friction at exactly the moment this audience expects to feel welcomed and efficient.
03
THE STAFF REALLOCATION
Faster Arrival Flow Gives Staff Back the Time to Do the Things That Drive Ancillary Revenue.
When front desk staff are processing a 25 to 30% faster check-in flow through contactless processes, the minutes they recover don't disappear — they become available for upsell conversations, restaurant recommendations, amenity introductions, and the human moments that drive ancillary spend. Speed at the desk isn't about replacing hospitality. It's about creating more time for the parts of it that move revenue.
04
THE REVIEW CORRELATION
Check-In Experience Is One of the Top-Cited Factors in Hotel Reviews — Positive and Negative.
Guests who experience a slow or frustrating check-in are significantly more likely to mention it in their review — and to let it color how they describe the entire stay. The inverse is also true: a smooth, fast arrival creates goodwill that carries forward. That goodwill shows up in review scores, which directly affect booking conversion on OTAs. Arrival speed is literally a distribution metric.

The 70% of hotel guests overall who say they prefer app or kiosk check-in aren't asking for a worse experience. They're asking for a different one — one where the transactional part of arrival is handled efficiently so the relational part can be more genuine. The properties that understand that distinction tend to score higher on both speed and warmth metrics, because their staff aren't buried in administrative processing when a guest walks in.

That's the paradox of hotel arrival speed: getting faster at the transactional moment doesn't make the stay feel less personal. Done right, it makes the personal moments easier to actually have.

WHERE THE SPEED OPPORTUNITY SITS IN YOUR PROPERTY RIGHT NOW
Four Arrival Moments That Are Slower Than Your Guests Expect.
CHECK-IN FLOW
The traditional front desk queue is the most visible friction point in your arrival experience. 70% of guests overall — and 82% of Gen Z guests — would prefer to bypass it entirely if a smooth digital option existed. Contactless check-in doesn't eliminate the front desk. It gives guests the choice, and that choice itself is part of the experience they're paying for.
GROUP ARRIVAL
Group arrivals — conferences, weddings, sports events — are where front desk bottlenecks become operationally critical. A hotel processing 40 rooms from the same group through a traditional front desk flow is creating a 45-minute queue for guests who already know each other and are comparing notes on how the arrival felt. Mobile pre-check-in for groups isn't a convenience feature. It's a reputation management strategy.
CHECKOUT SPEED
Checkout friction leaves a final impression that sticks. Contactless checkout processes save 4 to 6 minutes per interaction — but more importantly, they send guests off on a positive note instead of a queue-generated frustration. The last thing a guest experiences at your property is what they remember when they're deciding whether to rebook or recommend.
IN-STAY REQUESTS
Guests who can make in-stay requests digitally — housekeeping, F&B, amenity bookings — spend more and report higher satisfaction. The mechanism is access without friction. A guest who can order room service via a QR code instead of navigating a hold-heavy phone system is more likely to order. That ease of access is a direct driver of ancillary revenue per stay.

The hotels that are winning on both satisfaction and ancillary revenue aren't doing it by investing in more front desk staff. They're doing it by making the transactional moments fast enough that their existing staff can focus on the relational ones. Speed creates the space for hospitality. Without it, the whole stay starts behind.
