Summer Demand Is Experience-Led — How to Capture the Full Spend of the 2026 Travel Surge
SUMMER 2026 BRINGS THE BIGGEST TRAVEL DEMAND IN YEARS.
OCCUPANCY IS THE FLOOR. ANCILLARY REVENUE IS THE CEILING.

"Summer 2026 is a structural opportunity for hotels. The World Cup is bringing more international and domestic travelers through North America than any event in recent decades — and hotels remain the most popular accommodation choice for those travelers."

The summer of 2026 is not a typical summer for North American hospitality. The 2026 FIFA World Cup runs June 11 through July 19 — the first World Cup hosted in North America in 32 years, spread across 16 host cities and touching every adjacent market through fan travel, viewing events, and tourism spillover. The projected hospitality spend tied to that window — over $8.1 billion between June and August — represents the highest concentration of consumer experience spending this industry will see in years.

But the spending isn't driven by the tournament alone. The broader signal from 2026 consumer behavior is that people are still actively choosing to spend on experiences over goods — and they're increasingly selective about which experiences feel worth it. More than 40% of Gen Z World Cup trips are booked for 3 to 5 nights. These aren't day-trip attendees. They're guests with allocated budgets for dining, entertainment, and nightlife — and they're making spending decisions based primarily on how the experience feels from the moment they arrive.

The venues that capture disproportionate share of this summer's elevated demand won't be the ones with the best marketing. They'll be the ones where the experience is organized, frictionless, and worth talking about — because in 2026, a guest's social post about your venue is the most effective distribution channel you have.

WHY SUMMER 2026 IS THE MOST CONSEQUENTIAL DEMAND WINDOW IN RECENT HOSPITALITY HISTORY
Four Reasons This Summer's Guest Is Different — and What They're Spending On.
01
THE WORLD CUP WINDOW
The 2026 FIFA World Cup Generates the Largest Hotel Demand Surge in North American History.
The World Cup runs June 11 through July 19 across 16 North American host cities. Hotels in and near host markets will see occupancy and ADR pressures unlike anything since the pandemic recovery. But the opportunity extends beyond host cities: fan travel, tournament tourism, and the general elevation of summer travel spending will benefit properties across the continent during this window.
02
THE EXPERIENCE PREMIUM
Travelers Are Spending More on Experiences — and Hotels Are the Base Camp for All of Them.
The 2026 consumer travel signal is consistent: people are choosing experience-led trips with allocated budgets for dining, entertainment, and activities — not just accommodation. A hotel that positions itself as more than a place to sleep — as a curated base for the summer experience — captures more ancillary spend from guests who came to do things, not just stay somewhere.
03
THE GEN Z TRAVEL SURGE
Gen Z Travelers Are Your Fastest-Growing Segment — and They Have Specific Expectations.
More than 15% of Airbnb World Cup bookings are from Gen Z guests. That cohort — which overwhelmingly prefers digital check-in, digital service requests, and frictionless interactions — is choosing accommodation based on how the experience feels as much as where it is. Hotels that deliver a digital-first, frictionless experience are positioned to capture this segment's growing share of travel spend.
04
THE SPEND ENVIRONMENT
Hotels Remain the Most Popular Summer Accommodation Choice. The Opportunity Is Capturing Ancillary Spend Per Stay.
Despite the growth of alternative accommodation, hotels remain the most popular choice for summer travel — including World Cup travel. The question for hotel operators isn't occupancy. It's ancillary revenue per stay: how much of the spending that's coming through the door in summer 2026 is captured by your F&B, spa, amenity, and service offerings versus leaking to outside venues and delivery apps.

HOW TO POSITION YOUR VENUE FOR THE SUMMER 2026 EXPERIENCE DEMAND
Four Ways This Summer's Guests Are Deciding Where to Spend.
WORLD CUP GUESTS
Hotels near World Cup host cities are the accommodation anchor for the highest-spend traveler segment in North America this summer. Tournament guests have multi-day budgets that include accommodation, dining, entertainment, and experiences. Properties that make it easy to spend those budgets on-property — through digital F&B access, spa booking, curated local programming — capture a share of per-stay spend that otherwise leaks to outside venues.
MULTI-NIGHT STAYS
The 3–5 night World Cup trip is the primary booking profile for the summer's highest-spend travelers. Multi-night guests have more opportunities to engage with ancillary services — and more time to discover them if the discovery path is easy. Digital menus, digital amenity booking, and in-room experience guides that surface options guests didn't know existed are the mechanisms that convert multi-night stays into multi-night ancillary spend.
GEN Z TRAVELERS
82% of Gen Z hotel travelers prefer digital interactions — and this summer they represent an outsized share of total hotel bookings. Properties that deliver on the digital-first expectation — fast check-in, easy in-room ordering, frictionless service requests — earn the review scores and social posts that drive forward bookings. Properties that don't leave this segment slightly disappointed and underrepresented in the review pool.
ANCILLARY REVENUE
The gap between booked revenue and total revenue potential is the ancillary opportunity — and summer 2026 is when it's widest. Guests with elevated travel budgets during the World Cup window are looking to spend. F&B, spa, late checkout, premium room features, curated local experiences — these are all categories where the difference between capturing the spend and losing it to a delivery app is how easy the in-property option is to access.
