Guests Want Control, Not Chaos — Building the Fan Experience That Creates Loyalty
THE FAN WHO CONTROLLED THEIR EXPERIENCE COMES BACK.
THE ONE WHO FELT PROCESSED BY IT DOESN'T.

"The fan who felt in control of their experience — who ordered when they wanted, moved without friction, and got what they came for without waiting — is the fan who comes back. The one who felt managed and processed is the one who doesn't."

The hospitality industry has spent decades optimizing for a service model where the guest's comfort depends almost entirely on the speed and attention of staff. That model works — until the moment it doesn't. A slow night with attentive service is a great guest experience. A busy night where staff are stretched is a frustrating one. The variability is structural: guest satisfaction fluctuates with operational load rather than quality.

The shift that's been building across hospitality — and that's now measurable in guest preference data — is a growing desire for predictability and control over the service experience. Guests don't want to be managed. They want to be able to manage themselves. 70% of hotel guests prefer app or kiosk interactions for service requests. 82% of Gen Z guests prefer digital check-in. In food and beverage settings, guests who order digitally spend more — not because they're more enthusiastic about the brand, but because the experience gave them the control to make decisions without external pressure.

Self-service isn't a downgrade from hospitality. Done right, it's what hospitality looks like when it's built around the guest's pace rather than the operation's convenience. The venues that understand that distinction are the ones building loyalty with the guests who've been quietly frustrated by the alternative.

WHY GUEST CONTROL IS THE HIGHEST-RETURN EXPERIENCE VARIABLE IN 2026
Four Reasons Giving Guests More Control Raises Both Satisfaction and Revenue.
01
THE PREFERENCE SHIFT
75% of Fans Are Willing to Use Automated and Digital Service Options. They're Not Asking for Less Hospitality.
Three-quarters of guests in venue settings are willing to use digital ordering, self-service kiosks, and automated service paths. That's not a signal that fans want less engagement with your venue — it's a signal that they want more control over how and when that engagement happens. Digital service options give fans the choice. The experience of making the choice is itself a form of hospitality.
02
THE CONTROL PREMIUM
Fans Who Control Their Experience Spend More. The Research Is Consistent.
The 28% higher per-transaction spend that consistently shows up in mobile ordering data is partly a speed benefit — it's also a control benefit. A fan who orders on their own terms, without pressure, without a line behind them, makes larger and more deliberate decisions. Control over the ordering experience is the mechanism behind the check size increase. It's not incidental.
03
THE SATISFACTION LINK
Satisfaction Scores Rise When Fans Feel the Experience Is On Their Terms.
Guest satisfaction in a live event context is strongly correlated with whether guests felt in control of their experience — the ability to get what they wanted when they wanted it, without excessive dependence on staff availability. Digital ordering, fast pickup, and predictable service flows all contribute to that feeling. Venues that build control into the fan experience consistently score higher on satisfaction metrics than those that make every service interaction dependent on staff-to-fan timing.
04
THE STAFF EQUATION
Self-Service Frees Staff to Do the High-Value Work That Actually Drives Loyalty.
When fans can handle routine ordering and service requests digitally, staff are freed from the transactional processing that fills most service interactions. That time becomes available for the genuinely human moments — the interaction that makes a fan feel recognized, the upsell conversation that happens because the staff member isn't rushing to the next counter order, the experience moment that turns a first-time attendee into a regular. Self-service doesn't replace hospitality. It enables the parts of it that create loyalty.

WHERE GUEST CONTROL DELIVERS THE HIGHEST RETURN IN YOUR OPERATION
Four Experience Moments Where Predictability and Self-Service Raise Satisfaction and Spend.
IN-SEAT ORDERING
In-seat ordering is the highest-control experience a stadium can offer — and consistently the one fans most want when given the choice. A fan who can order from their seat without leaving, without timing a line visit around the action, and without missing a moment has been given exactly the kind of control that drives satisfaction and return attendance. The venues that make in-seat ordering visible and accessible are building loyalty with every order placed from the stands.
PICKUP PREDICTABILITY
Predictable pickup — knowing the order will be ready in 5 minutes and it will be correct — is as important as ordering speed for fan satisfaction. Fans who trust the pickup process use it more. Fans who've had one bad pickup experience revert to the counter queue because it feels more in their control. Getting the pickup experience right — consistently, every game — is what converts first-time mobile orderers into habitual ones.
WAYFINDING & INFO
Fans who know where things are feel more in control of their experience — and more positive about the venue. Clear digital wayfinding, real-time capacity information, and in-app venue guides that tell fans what's available and where to find it reduce the anxiety of being in an unfamiliar environment. That reduction in environmental anxiety directly contributes to spending more freely and feeling better about the experience.
LOYALTY CAPTURE
The fan who had a high-control, high-satisfaction experience is the one who is ready to join a loyalty program. Digital ordering creates a natural capture moment: the fan is already in a digital interface, already having a positive experience, already predisposed toward engagement. A loyalty enrollment prompt at the moment of a successful order converts at dramatically higher rates than a cold sign-up push at entry. Control-first design creates loyalty-ready guests.

The fan experience that builds loyalty isn't the one where everything is done for the guest. It's the one where the guest feels like the venue was designed around how they want to experience it. That means control over ordering, predictability in service, and digital access to everything the venue offers. Fans who feel that way come back. Fans who feel managed and processed find somewhere else.
