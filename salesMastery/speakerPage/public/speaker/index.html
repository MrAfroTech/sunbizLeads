Release Date: May 3, 2026

Chaos Mastery is written for hospitality operators — restaurant owners, bar managers, venue directors, hotel operators, and event producers. The operators who thrive are not the ones who react quickly to chaos — they are the ones who build systems that prevent chaos from happening in the first place. The book spans 4,000 years of hospitality history, from ancient Rome to AI-powered intelligent venues of 2026, documenting how every era's top operators mastered their moment by treating their venue as a living system rather than a series of daily emergencies. It also addresses the modern operational pain points that drain revenue and staff — long wait times, disjointed tech stacks, and overworked teams — showing how smart systems can reduce stress, streamline operations, and make running a venue far more predictable and profitable. Readers will learn how to optimize workflows, design environments that guide guest behavior naturally, leverage technology without creating chaos, and ultimately create venues that function efficiently even under pressure, turning unpredictable days into manageable, repeatable successes.

About Maurice

A serial entrepreneur with 27 years of experience across sales, logistics, publishing, and technology, he has spent his career identifying inefficiencies and building systems that work. As a guest, he’s seen firsthand the frustrations and bottlenecks in hospitality — long lines, inconsistent service, and disjointed operations — and has made it his life’s mission to optimize leisure time while creating better work environments for those who serve.

Driven by this mission, he has conducted extensive research into the history of hospitality, tracing 4,000 years of evolution from the bustling taverns of ancient Rome to the AI-powered intelligent venues of today. His research reveals how top operators across eras mastered their venues by treating them as living systems rather than reacting to daily emergencies. By studying these historical insights, he highlights timeless principles of operational design, guest experience, and staff management that continue to shape how hospitality functions in modern times.
