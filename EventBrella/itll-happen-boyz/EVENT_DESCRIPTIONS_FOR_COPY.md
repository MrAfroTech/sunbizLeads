# Event Descriptions for Copy Writing - Farmer Banks Harvest Experience

## Overview
Farmer Banks Helfrich hosts hands-on harvest experiences at his farm in Clermont, Florida. These events combine education, community, and the joy of harvesting fresh produce directly from the source.

---

## Event Types

### 1. Monthly Harvest Experience
**Frequency:** 12 events per year (second Sunday of each month)  
**Time:** 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM EST  
**Duration:** 2 hours  
**Price:** $10.00 per ticket  
**Location:** Here On The Farm, 9100 Sams Lake Road, Clermont, FL 34715

**What It Is:**
A recurring monthly event where participants join Farmer Banks for a hands-on harvest experience. Each month features different crops and seasonal activities based on what's ready to harvest.

**Activities Included:**
- **Hands-On Harvesting:** Learn to harvest fresh produce directly from the farm
- **Bring Your Own Shovel:** Participants bring their own tools and get hands-on experience digging and harvesting
- **Farmer Tours:** Guided tour of the farm with Farmer Banks explaining sustainable farming practices, crop cycles, and what makes each harvest special
- **Cooking Tips:** Learn how to prepare and cook the harvested produce with tips from Farmer Banks
- **Fresh Air & Community:** Enjoy the outdoors, meet fellow harvesters, and experience the farm community

**What Makes It Special:**
- Monthly variety - different crops each month based on season
- Educational component - learn about sustainable farming
- Community-focused - bring friends and family
- Affordable at $10 per person
- Hands-on experience - not just watching, but actively participating

**What to Bring:**
- Your own shovel (essential tool)
- Comfortable clothes (you'll get dirty)
- Water bottle
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
- Enthusiasm for getting your hands dirty!

**Atmosphere:**
Fun, educational, community-oriented. Expect laughter, fresh air, good company, and the satisfaction of harvesting your own food.

## Tone & Style Guidelines

### Voice
- **Friendly and welcoming** - like a neighbor inviting you over
- **Educational but not preachy** - informative without being condescending
- **Enthusiastic** - genuine excitement about farming and harvesting
- **Accessible** - not intimidating for beginners
- **Community-focused** - emphasizes connection and shared experience

### Writing Style
- Use active voice: "Harvest fresh produce" not "Fresh produce will be harvested"
- Be specific: "Dig up cassava roots" not "harvest vegetables"
- Include sensory details: "Feel the earth, smell the fresh produce, taste the results"
- Use inclusive language: "Everyone is welcome" "All skill levels"
- Emphasize the experience: "Join us" "Experience" "Learn" "Discover"

### Key Phrases to Use
- "Hands-on harvest experience"
- "Learn from Farmer Banks"
- "Sustainable farming practices"
- "Fresh from the farm"
- "Community of harvesters"
- "Root to table"
- "Get your hands dirty"
- "Bring your own shovel"
- "Seasonal variety"
- "Educational and fun"

### What to Avoid
- Overly technical farming jargon (unless explaining it)
- Making it sound like hard labor (it's fun work!)
- Exclusivity language (these are welcoming events)
- Generic farm tour language (these are specific harvest experiences)
